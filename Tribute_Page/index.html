Steve Jobs

The Visionary Co-founder of Apple Inc. and Pixar Animation

The Life and Timeline of Steve Jobs

Steven Paul Jobs was an American businessman, inventor, and industrial designer. He was the co-founder, chairman, and CEO of Apple Inc. Through his revolutionary products like the iPhone, iPod, and Macintosh, he completely reshaped the personal computer, music, and mobile industries.

  • 1955 - Born in San Francisco, California, and adopted by Paul and Clara Jobs.
  • 1976 - Co-founded Apple Computer with his friend Steve Wozniak inside his parents' garage.
  • 1984 - Introduced the original Macintosh, the first mass-market computer with a graphical interface.
  • 1985 - Forced out of Apple after a power struggle. He then founded NeXT computers and bought Pixar Animation.
  • 1997 - Returned to Apple as CEO when Apple acquired NeXT, saving the company from near bankruptcy.
  • 2001 - Launched the iPod, changing the entire digital music industry forever.
  • 2007 - Unveiled the original iPhone, completely transforming how humans interact with technology.
  • 2011 - Passed away in Palo Alto, California, leaving behind an unforgettable legacy of innovation.
